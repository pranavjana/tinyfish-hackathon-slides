## Slide 2: TinyFish Recap
"Quick recap for anyone who missed it — TinyFish builds end-to-end infrastructure for AI agents to operate the web. Search, browser, content extraction, autonomous agent — all in one platform."

## Slide 3: Your Browser Today (INTERACTIVE)
"Think about everything you did on your browser today. Email. Slack. Filing forms. Comparing prices. Booking flights."

*Pause. Let them think.*

"What if all of that could be run by an agent — in the cloud — in seconds?"

"Imagine the collective amount of browser activity across all white-collar workers. All of that can be accelerated, virtualised, running in the cloud."

> **Extra context:** "Not everything you do can be done by an agent today. But we're in the early days. Think about it like GPT-2 — not many people knew about GPT-2, but everyone knows GPT-4. It's already proven. We're at that GPT-2 moment for web agents."

---

## Slide 4: It's Already Happening
"This is not hypothetical. 51% of web traffic is already bots. AI traffic grew 188% in 2025 alone."

"The internet is shifting from something built for humans to something built for agents."

---

## Slide 5: The Web Is Being Rebuilt for Agents
"Look at what's happening right now. Sites are adding llms.txt files so agents can understand their content. Services are shipping MCP servers so agents can plug in natively. Structured APIs everywhere."

"The infrastructure for an agent-native web is being laid — right now. This is not coming. It's here."

"So what does this mean?"

---

## Slide 6: Web Agent Moment Will Be Bigger
"Coding agents already changed the flow of the world — hiring changed, capital flows changed, product velocity changed. And that was a market of only about 30 million developers."

*Point at the scale bars.*

"Browsing? That's everyone. Every white-collar worker on earth. 5.5 billion people. The web agent moment will dwarf the coding agent moment."

"There will be a moment for web agents like the coding agent moment. And when it hits, it will be bigger — because the market is bigger."

> **Extra context:** Ask the crowd: "What was the impact of coding agents? What changed in hiring, in capital, in product?" Get 2-3 answers. Then land on: "And coding is exclusive to a few million people on the planet. For browsing — that's everyone."

## Slide 7: Trillion-Dollar Question
*Let this land. Pause after the quote.*

"How much are people paying for tax filing? Insurance comparison? HR onboarding? That's already happening."

"Do you think trillions of dollars will continue to be paid for people to click buttons and fill forms?"

"This part of the economy can be automated, virtualised, all in the cloud. The capital will flow into things that actually matter."

> **Extra context:** "That's already happening for insurance. Total disruption. We can't imagine the pre-GPT-2 era anymore. It's getting more and more specific — example after example of industries being automated."

## Slide 8: The Provocation
"Video generation is already a billion-dollar industry. When you can make a movie at Oscar-level acting for $20 overnight — and it's essentially the same quality — would you still pay millions for that? The tech will get there."

"When robots can fold your clothes, when AI can make Oscar-level movies... imagine still clicking buttons yourself."

"Web operations is the largest addressable market. Not just developers — every knowledge worker needs it."

> **Extra context:** "The capital will flow into more things that matter. This part of the economy can be automated, virtualised, all in the cloud."

## Slide 9: What Can't Be Automated? (INTERACTIVE)
*Take 2-3 answers from the audience. Challenge each one gently.*

"What do you think can't be automated by web agents?"

"Are you sure? What people thought couldn't be done by coding agents 2 years ago is now routine."

"Think hard about what people are being paid to do right now that can't be automated. The list is shrinking fast."

> **Extra context:** The goal here is to have them really believe in this future. They should want to make a bet. This is where the capital will go. You can build a solution that catches this capital — a solution for certain use cases where money is being spent today, and that money will come to you.

## Slide 10: GPT-2 Analogy
"We're in the GPT-2 era of web agents. The tech is emerging — promising, but clunky. Not quite there yet."

"Not many people knew about GPT-2. Everyone knows GPT-4. That was the moment it just worked — undeniable."

"Web agents are feasible. Already showing they work. Now it's about distribution. Like Boston Dynamics 10 years ago — look at it now."

> **Extra context:** "We can't imagine the pre-GPT-2 era anymore. The same will happen for web agents. The GPT-4 moment is coming."

---

## Slide 11: Illegal for Humans to Drive
"Elon predicted it will be illegal for humans to drive. The same will happen for the web."

"It's going to require approval for humans to touch the web. Trust AI to do the part."

"Why? Humans make mistakes. Agents can validate their own actions — internalise that 100 times before doing something."

"The hardest threshold: making agents make fewer mistakes than humans. We're getting there."

> **Extra context:** "The hardest challenge is adoption. Similar to autonomous driving. But once agents consistently make fewer mistakes than humans, the threshold is crossed. And then it flips — it becomes irresponsible to NOT use agents."

---

## Slide 12: From Scrapers to Agents
"A common misconception: TinyFish is a scraper. Let me debunk that."

"Scrapers are Gen 1 — dumb, brittle, break when HTML changes. Browser automation is Gen 2 — can interact, but no intelligence, you script every step. TinyFish is Gen 3 — AI agents that understand, navigate, decide, and act. They adapt when things change."

"Fundamentally different. Like comparing a horse-drawn cart to a self-driving car."
