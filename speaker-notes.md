# Speaker Notes — The Future of the Web

## Slide 1: Title
*No notes — let the title breathe.*

## Slide 2: Your Browser Today (INTERACTIVE)
Let them sit with this. Pause. "Think about everything you did on your browser today. Email. Slack. Forms. Price comparison. Booking."
Then: "What if all of that could run by an agent?"

## Slide 3: It's Already Happening
*No notes — the stats speak for themselves.*

## Slide 4: Coding Agent Moment (INTERACTIVE)
Ask the crowd: "What happened when coding agents arrived? What changed?"
Get 2-3 answers. Guide toward: hiring, capital, product velocity.
Then land on: "And coding is exclusive to a few million people on the planet."

## Slide 5: Web Agent > Coding Agent
*Let the scale bars land visually. Point at the difference.*

## Slide 6: Trillion-Dollar Question
Let this land. Pause. "How much are people paying for tax filing? Insurance comparison? That's already happening."
"This part of the economy can be automated, virtualised, all in the cloud. The capital will flow into things that actually matter."

## Slide 7: The Provocation
"Video generation is already a billion-dollar industry. When you can make a movie at Oscar-level for $20 overnight, would you still pay millions?"
"The tech will get there. And web operations is a bigger market than all of those."

## Slide 8: What Can't Be Automated? (INTERACTIVE)
Take 2-3 answers from the audience. Challenge each one gently.
"Are you sure? Remember: not many people knew about GPT-2. Then GPT-4 arrived and it was already proven."
"What people thought couldn't be done by coding agents 2 years ago is now routine."

## Slide 9: GPT-2 Analogy
*"GPT-2 was promising but rough — few people took it seriously. Then GPT-4 arrived and it just worked. Undeniable. Web agents are at that GPT-2 moment right now. The GPT-4 moment is coming."*

## Slide 10: Illegal for Humans to Drive
"Elon predicted it will be illegal for humans to drive. The same will happen for the web."
"It's going to require approval for humans to touch the web. Why? Because agents validate 100x before doing something. Humans can't."

## Slide 11: TinyFish Intro
*Quick transition. "Now let me show you what we're building."*

## Slide 12: The Four Primitives
"Search, fetch, browsing, agent — these are implementation details. It's a really simple decision."
"It's an objective-oriented system. You give the agent a goal, and it decides what primitives to use."

## Slide 13: Why TinyFish
"We're rethinking how to 'web'. Microsecond-level page manipulation. Stream compiled pages into GPU."
"Imagine a future where the only bottleneck is your network latency, and verification."

## Slide 14: The Bet (CLOSE)
"We're at the very early days. Like Boston Dynamics 10 years ago. But we're really accelerating."
"TinyFish is the best partner and infrastructure to bet on. Build a solution for use cases where money is being spent today — and that money will come to you."
